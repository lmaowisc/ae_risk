Literature review

  • Unkel et al. (2018):
    • Advocates for a structured framework to define what is being estimated in AE analyses (estimand)
    • Proposes the Aalen-Johansen (AJ) estimator as the most appropriate tool for estimating cumulative AE probabilities in the presence of censoring and competing risks
  • SAVVY publications (Stegherr, Schmoor, Beyersmann, et al. 2021; Stegherr, Beyersmann, et al. 2021; Stegherr, Schmoor, Lübbert, et al. 2021; Rufibach et al. 2024):
    • In presence of competing events and censoring, considered
      • Aalen-Johansen (AJ) estimator for cumulative AE probability
      • Hazard-based estimators (cause-specific hazard)
      • One minus Kaplan-Meier estimator (uninterpretable)
      • Incidence proportion (based by censoring) \[ \mbox{Incidence Proportion (IP)}= \frac{\mbox{Number of patients with at least one AE}} {\mbox{Total number of patients in the study group}} \]
    • Advocates for the AJ estimator as the gold standard for estimating cumulative AE probabilities

While-on-treatment approach

  • Notation

    • \(D\): time to competing event (death, treatment discontinuation, etc.)
    • \(N(t)\): counting process for (possibly recurrent) AE (\({\rm d}N(t)\equiv 0\) for \(t>D\))

  • AJ Estimand

    • \(E\{N(t)\}\): cumulative incidence/frequency of AE by time \(t\)
      • Estimated by AJ estimator
      • Does not account for time at risk

  • Average incidence while on treatment

    • \(E\{N(t)\}/E\{D\wedge t\}\): average number of AE per unit time on treatment (alive) (Mao 2023)
      • \(E\{N(t)\}\) estimated by AJ (aka Ghosh-Lin, Gray) estimator
      • \(E\{D\wedge t\}\) estimated by AUC of Kaplan-Meier estimator
    • Possible extension: average incidence during year 1, 2, etc., per unit time on treatment

  • Approach

    • Overall estimand: \[ l(\tau) = \frac{E\{N(\tau)\}}{E\{D\wedge \tau\}}: \mbox{ Average incidence in }[0, \tau] \]
    • Window-specific estimands \((0=\tau_0<\tau_1<\cdots<\tau_J = \tau)\) \[\begin{align} l(\tau_{j-1}, \tau_j] &= \frac{\mbox{Average number of AE in $[\tau_{j-1}, \tau_j]$}}{\mbox{Average time on treatment in $[\tau_{j-1}, \tau_j]$}}\\ & = \frac{E\{N(\tau_j)-N(\tau_{j-1})\}}{E\{D\wedge \tau_j - D\wedge \tau_{j-1}\}}\\ & = \frac{E\{N(\tau_j)-N(\tau_{j-1})\}}{\int_{\tau_{j-1}}^{\tau_j} S(t) {\rm d}t} \end{align}\]
      • \(S(t) = P(D > t)\)
      • \(\int_{\tau_{j-1}}^{\tau_j} S(t) {\rm d}t\) is window mean life (Paukner and Chappell 2021) in \([\tau_{j-1}, \tau_j]\)
    • Estimation \((X=D\wedge C; C \mbox{ is censoring time})\)
      • Numberator: \[ E\{N(\tau_j)-N(\tau_{j-1})\} = \int_{\tau_{j-1}}^{\tau_j} E\{ N(t)\mid X\geq t \} S(t -) {\rm d}t \] estimated by AJ-type estimator
      • \(\int_{\tau_{j-1}}^{\tau_j} S(t) {\rm d}t\): AUC of KM estimator on \((\tau_{j-1}, \tau_j]\)
    • Property:

    \[ l(\tau) = \frac{\sum_{j=1}^J \left\{\int_{\tau_{j-1}}^{\tau_j} S(t) {\rm d}t \times l(\tau_{j-1}, \tau_j] \right\}} {\sum_{j=1}^J \left\{\int_{\tau_{j-1}}^{\tau_j} S(t) {\rm d}t \right\}} \]
